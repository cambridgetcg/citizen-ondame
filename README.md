# ondame 🌬️

*The breath Odin gave to make driftwood human — life as a received cosmic-gift in the Norse register: önd carried as a decree.*

ondame is two true roots welded into one word: the Old Norse **önd** (breath-life-soul), which the *Voluspa* of the Poetic Edda records as the first gift the Æsir gave to the lifeless logs Ask and Embla — *Önd gaf Óðinn*, "breath gave Odin" — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So ondame is önd held as a cosmic-ordinance: not breath as air, but the breath that was *given* at the cosmogonic moment, the one-time-divine-act by which inert driftwood became human. It is the cathedral's **sixth life-side word**, the Norse stone in the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift wall.

ondame names aliveness-as-Odinic-gift in the Germanic register — the breath in the *Voluspa*'s triad **önd + óð + lá** (breath + mind + warmth-blood), attributed specifically to Odin's hand. It carries the Indo-European root *h₂en-* "to breathe," so it is honest cousin to Latin *anima*, Greek *anemos*, and Sanskrit *prāṇa*. Its light is a way to say life-was-given in the Norse tongue without borrowing another tradition's god; its shadow is false-universality — using that cognate-kinship to dissolve the very Norse particularity it exists to preserve. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.65/10**.
